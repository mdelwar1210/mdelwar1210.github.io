SBUEats

Project Overview: SBUEats is an innovative mobile app designed to revolutionize the on-campus dining experience at Stony Brook University. The project aimed to address the limitations of existing dining applications by providing a streamlined and user-friendly solution that consolidates various functionalities into a single, intuitive platform.


Problem Statement: The project aimed to address the challenges faced by students in accessing dining information, placing orders, and managing payments effectively within the campus ecosystem.


Research and Analysis: Conducted user interviews and analyzed existing campus dining systems to identify pain points and opportunities for improvement.


Design Process & Methodology: In the design process for SBUEats, a user-centered design approach was followed, incorporating Gestalt's principles of design and usability heuristics. Feedback from peers at Stony Brook University who regularly use dining apps informed the design decisions and iterative improvements. The aim was to create an intuitive and user-friendly experience by ensuring consistency, visibility, user control, and a minimalist design approach.


Key Features and Solutions: Implemented features such as real-time menus, personalized recommendations, easy ordering and customization options, and secure payment integration to streamline the dining experience for students.


Visual Design: The SBUEats interface is designed with a carefully selected color scheme that aligns with Stony Brook University's branding, emphasizing a visually appealing and cohesive experience. The Roboto font is used for enhanced readability, while the application of Gestalt's principles ensures visual organization and allows users to quickly locate and process information. The interface prioritizes intuitive navigation, minimalistic design, and user-centered principles.
